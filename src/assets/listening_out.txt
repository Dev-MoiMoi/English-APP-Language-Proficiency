ALISTENING

This section offers various activities designed to develop your listening skills. Practicing listening helps improve both your understanding of the language and your pronunciation.
The self-study lessons are carefully structured according to English proficiency levels based on the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR). You will encounter audio recordings from different real-life situations, along with interactive exercises that strengthen the listening skills needed for academic success, professional growth, and everyday communication. The speakers come from diverse national backgrounds, reflecting how English is used globally today.
You may also take a free online English test to determine your appropriate level. Choose from A1 (elementary) to C1 (advanced) and enhance your listening abilities at your own pace, anytime that suits your schedule.

A1 LISTENING
Listening practice to help you understand familiar words and basic phrases when people speak slowly and clearly. Situations include meeting people, shopping and conversations at work!

A2 LISTENING
Listening practice to help you understand common vocabulary and expressions in short, clear dialogues. Situations include simple explanations, introductions, messages and announcements.

B1 LISTENING
Listening practice to help you understand the main points of clear, standard speech about everyday or job-related topics. Situations include phone calls, meetings and interviews.

B2 LISTENING
Listening practice to help you understand extended, standard speech about familiar topics that may contain complex ideas. Situations include broadcasts, reviews, presentations and lectures.

C1 - C2 LISTENING
Listening practice to help you understand extended speech about abstract, complex or unfamiliar topics. Situations include job interviews, lectures, talks and meetings.
