Start writing with purpose.
Practice tasks that help you turn your ideas into clear and organized writing, from short messages to formal essays.

A1
Task 1: Write a short paragraph (3–5 sentences) about yourself.
Include:
• Your name 
• Your age 
• Where you live 
• Your favorite hobby

Task 2: My Favorite Food
Write 3–5 sentences.
Include:
• What it is 
• What color it is 
• Why you like it 

Task 3: My School
Write 3–5 sentences.
Include:
• Name of your school 
• What you see there 
• What you like

Task 4: My Best Friend
Write 3–5 sentences.
Include:
•​Their name
•​What they look like (tall/short, hair color)
•​What you do together

A2
Task 1: Write a short message (5–7 sentences) to a friend about your daily routine.
Include:
• What time you wake up 
• What you do in the morning 
• Your school or work activities 
• What you do in the evening

Task 2: My Last Holiday
Write 5–7 sentences.
Include:
• Where you went 
• What you did 
• Who you were with 

Task 3: My Favorite Place
Write 5–7 sentences.
Include:
• Where it is 
• What you can do there 
• Why you like it

Task 4: A Typical Weekend
Write 5–7 sentences.
Include:
•​What time you get up on Saturday
•​Where you go or who you see
•​One thing you usually buy or eat
•​How you feel on Sunday evening

B1
Task 1: Write a paragraph (8–10 sentences) about a memorable experience in your life.
Include:
• Where and when it happened 
• Who you were with 
• What happened 
• Why it was memorable

Task 2: My Future Plans
Write 8–10 sentences.
Include:
• Your goals 
• What you will do 
• Why it is important 

Task 3: Advantages and Disadvantages
Topic: Using the internet for learning
Write 8–10 sentences.

Task 4: Advice for a Healthy Life 
Write 8–10 sentences.
Include:
•​What people should eat and drink
•​The importance of physical activity
•​Mental health or sleep advice
•​A concluding thought on why being healthy is difficult today

B2
Task 1: Write an essay (120–150 words)
Topic: Do social media platforms have more advantages or disadvantages?
Include:
• Your opinion 
• At least two reasons 
• Examples to support your ideas

Task 2: Opinion Essay (120–150 words)
Topic: Is it better to study alone or in groups?

Task 3: Problem-Solution Essay (120–150 words)
Topic: Students are always tired.
• Causes 
• Solutions

Task 4: Review of a Recent Purchase
 Write an essay/review (120–150 words) on a product you bought recently (e.g., a phone, a book, or clothes).
Include:
•​A description of the product
•​The pros and cons of using it
•Whether it was worth the money
•​Who you would recommend it to

C1
Task1: Write an argumentative essay (180–220 words) 
Topic: Should education be fully online in the future?
Include:
• A clear thesis statement 
• Arguments for and against 
• Your conclusion with justification

Task 2: Formal Report
Topic: Write a report about student performance in your class.
Include:
• Observations 
• Problems 
• Recommendations 

Task 3: Review Writing
Write a review of a movie, book, or place.
Include:
• Summary 
• Opinion 
• Recommendation

Task 4: Proposal for a Community Project
Write a formal proposal (180–220 words) for a project to improve your local neighborhood.
Include:
•​A description of the current issue
•​Detailed steps for the proposed project
•​Expected benefits for the community
•​Potential challenges and how to overcome them

C2
Task1: Write a critical essay (250–300 words) analyzing the statement:
Technology is reshaping human relationships for better or worse.
Include:
• A nuanced argument 
• Critical evaluation of both perspectives 
• Sophisticated vocabulary and complex sentence structures

Task 2: Critical Essay (250–300 words)
Topic: Education should focus more on skills than knowledge.

Task 3: Reflective Essay (250–300 words)
Topic: A life lesson that changed your perspective.

Task 4: Philosophical Analysis 
Write a critical essay (250–300 words) discussing the following:
The concept of privacy is an obsolete relic in the digital age.
Include:
•​An evaluation of how data surveillance impacts individual autonomy.
•​A discussion on the trade-off between security and personal freedom.
•​Synthesis of opposing sociological viewpoints using highly sophisticated discourse markers and precise terminology.
