Ready to speak with confidence?
Explore speaking tasks that help you express your ideas clearly, from basic conversations to advanced critical discussions. Step by step, level by level.

A1
Task 1: Name and Show
Show or imagine 3 objects (e.g., phone, book, bag) 
Say: 
• What it is 
• Its color 
• If you like it

Task 2: My Favorite Things
Instructions: Talk about your favorite things (1–2 minutes).
You can include:
• Your favorite food 
• Your favorite color 
• Your favorite place 
• Your favorite activity

Task 3: My Family Tree 
•Identify 3 members of your family.
•​Say their names and their relationship to you (e.g., "This is my mother, Maria").
•​State one simple thing they like (e.g., "She likes coffee").

​Task 4: Daily Weather and Clothes 
•Look outside or imagine a season.
•​Say what the weather is like (e.g., "It is sunny/cold").
•​Say what you are wearing today.

A2
Task1: Describe Your Friend or Family Member
• Who is the person? 
• What do they look like? 
• What do they like doing? 

Task 2: Talking About Your Weekend Plans
Instructions: Talk about what you will do this weekend (2–3 minutes).
Include:
• Where you will go 
• Who you will be with 
• What activities you will do 
• Why you are excited (or not)

Task 3: Describe Your Home
• How many rooms? 
• What is in your house? 
• Your favorite part of the house

Task 4 : My Favorite Day
• What is your favorite day? 
• What do you usually do? 
• Why do you like it?

B1
Task 1: Short presentation
Topic: “My favorite place”
• Where is it? 
• Why do you like it? 
• What do you do there?

Task 2: Talk About a Past Activity
• What did you do last weekend? 
• Where did you go? 
• Who were you with? 
• Did you enjoy it? Why/why not?

Task 3: Planning a Trip 
•Talk about a place you want to visit in the future.
•​Explain how you will get there and where you will stay.
•​Describe at least two things you plan to see or do.

​Task 4: My Learning Journey 
•Describe when you started learning English.
•​Talk about what you find easy and what you find difficult.
•​Explain why you want to be fluent.

B2
Task 1: Give advice
Scenario: Your friend wants to improve their English. What advice will you give?
Include:
• At least 2 suggestions 
• Reasons for each

Task 2: Compare Two Things
Topic: Online class vs Face-to-face class
• Similarities 
• Differences 
• Which do you prefer? Why?

Task 3: The Impact of Advertising 
•Discuss how advertisements influence people's buying habits.
•​Give an example of a time you were persuaded to buy something.
•​Share your opinion on whether advertising to children should be banned.

​Task 4: Environmental Responsibility 
•Describe what you or your community do to help the environment (e.g., recycling, saving water).
•​Discuss the challenges of living a "green" lifestyle.
•​Suggest one major change the government should make to protect nature.

C1
Task 1: Compare and Evaluate Ideas
Topic: Online learning vs Traditional classroom learning
Instructions:
•Compare both methods
•Discuss advantages and disadvantages
•Decide which is more effective and explain why

Task 2: Problem-Solving Discussion
Scenario: Your school wants to reduce student stress.
• Suggest solutions 
• Explain why they will work 
• Evaluate the best option

Task 3: Ethics in the Modern World 
•Discuss a specific ethical dilemma (e.g., data privacy vs. national security or animal testing for medicine).
•​Analyze the conflicting interests of the parties involved.
•​Justify your personal stance using nuanced language.

​Task 4: Interpreting a Statement
Statement: Success is more about hard work than talent.
Instructions:
•Explain the meaning of the statement
•Give real-life examples
•Say whether you agree or disagree and why

C2
Task 1: Make a balanced argument
Topic: “Should technology replace teachers?”
Include:
• Both sides 
• Evaluation 
• Final opinion

Task 2: Abstract Topic Discussion
Topic: “Success means different things to different people.”
• Explain the idea 
• Give examples 
• Analyze different perspectives 
• Give your own interpretation

Task 3: The Concept of Globalization 
•Critically analyze the socio-economic impacts of globalization on local cultures.
•​Discuss whether the world is becoming too "homogenized."
•​Provide a sophisticated argument regarding the balance between global progress and cultural preservation.

​Task 4: Philosophical Inquiry: Determinism vs. Free Will 
•Discuss the extent to which human life is shaped by external circumstances versus personal choice.
•​Synthesize perspectives from psychology, sociology, or philosophy.
•​Engage in a high-level defense of your worldview using complex rhetorical strategies.
